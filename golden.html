General Characteristics

Golden retrievers are medium to large-sized dogs with a strong, muscular build and a dense, water-repellent double coat usually in shades of golden. They typically weigh 55 to 75 pounds and stand 21.5 to 24 inches tall at the shoulder. Their expressive eyes, gentle temperament, and friendly demeanor make them highly recognizable.

Health Considerations

Golden retrievers are generally healthy but are prone to certain conditions, including hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, heart issues, cataracts, and cancer. Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, and maintaining a healthy weight can prevent or manage many health issues. Life expectancy ranges around 10-12 years. Responsible breeding practices can reduce the risk of genetic diseases.
